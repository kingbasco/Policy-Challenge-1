The Challenge

About the Policy Challenge

The Policy Challenge is a national competition organised to empower the next generation of policy thinkers and problem-solvers. It brings together students, researchers, professionals, and innovators who are passionate about shaping practical solutions to Nigeria’s most pressing challenges.


The initiative invites undergraduate and postgraduate students, early-career professionals, researchers, civil society practitioners, public sector innovators, and policy think tanks to engage in rigorous policy thinking and collaborative problem-solving. Participants develop evidence-based proposals that address real governance, economic, and development issues while gaining exposure to the processes that shape public policy.


Through the competition, participants strengthen their ability to analyse complex problems, design implementable policy solutions, and communicate ideas that can influence decision-making. The Policy Challenge also provides opportunities for mentorship, collaboration, and engagement with experts working across policy, governance, academia, and development.


By creating a space where emerging voices can contribute meaningful ideas, the Policy Challenge aims to bridge the gap between policy design and real-world implementation, while cultivating a new generation of leaders equipped to shape Nigeria’s future.

The Theme 2026

Why It Matters

Across Nigeria, public trust in institutions has been tested by governance challenges, economic pressures, and concerns about accountability and service delivery. For democratic systems to function effectively, citizens must believe that institutions act in their interest, deliver public value, and operate transparently.


Rebuilding trust in the Nigerian state requires thoughtful institutional reforms that strengthen legitimacy, improve effectiveness, and ensure governance is inclusive and responsive to all citizens. From improving public service delivery to strengthening accountability mechanisms, institutional change is critical to restoring confidence in governance.


Possible Policy Areas

Participants may explore proposals in areas such as, but not limited to:

 Public sector and civil service reform

 Transparency, accountability, and anti-corruption

 Electoral integrity and democratic governance

 Digital governance and open government

 Judicial reforms and access to justice

 Citizen participation and civic engagement

 Local government strengthening and decentralisation

 Youth inclusion in governance

The Policy Challenge empowers young Nigerians to develop practical, research-based solutions to governance and development issues while gaining visibility, mentorship, and national recognition.
