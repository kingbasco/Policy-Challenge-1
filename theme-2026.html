The Theme 2026

Why It Matters

Across Nigeria, public trust in institutions has been tested by governance challenges, economic pressures, and concerns about accountability and service delivery. For democratic systems to function effectively, citizens must believe that institutions act in their interest, deliver public value, and operate transparently.


Rebuilding trust in the Nigerian state requires thoughtful institutional reforms that strengthen legitimacy, improve effectiveness, and ensure governance is inclusive and responsive to all citizens. From improving public service delivery to strengthening accountability mechanisms, institutional change is critical to restoring confidence in governance.


Possible Policy Areas

Participants may explore proposals in areas such as, but not limited to:

 Public sector and civil service reform

 Transparency, accountability, and anti-corruption

 Electoral integrity and democratic governance

 Digital governance and open government

 Judicial reforms and access to justice

 Citizen participation and civic engagement

 Local government strengthening and decentralisation

 Youth inclusion in governance

The Policy Challenge empowers young Nigerians to develop practical, research-based solutions to governance and development issues while gaining visibility, mentorship, and national recognition.
